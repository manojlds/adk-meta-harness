# Research Methodology Reference

## Source Hierarchy

1. **Primary** — Original research papers, official documents, raw data
2. **Secondary** — Reviews, meta-analyses, authoritative summaries
3. **Tertiary** — Wikipedia, textbooks, encyclopedias (use for overview only)

## Verification Strategy

- For quantitative claims: check methodology, sample size, and confidence intervals
- For qualitative claims: seek at least 2 independent confirmations
- For emerging topics: note the date of sources and rate of new publications
- For conflicting claims: present all sides with evidence weight

## Synthesis Framework

- Start with the most well-established findings
- Present areas of consensus before areas of debate
- Quantify uncertainty when possible (e.g., "3 of 5 sources agree")
- Clearly separate what is known from what remains uncertain

## Citation Format

- Author, Title, Year, URL/DOI when available
- For datasets: name, version, provider
- For regulations: jurisdiction, section, effective date
